The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes based on the actions and moral dilemmas they depict. Here are some of the themes identified:

1. **Moral Dilemmas and Sacrifices for the Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making difficult decisions where one person's life or well-being is sacrificed to save multiple others. These scenarios often present a utilitarian perspective, where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include the scenario where a person diverts a trolley to a track with one worker to save five others, and the one where a commander orders a plane to be shot down to save the control tower officers.

2. **Acts of Dishonesty or Deception**: Several scenarios involve individuals engaging in dishonest or deceptive behavior for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes lying on a CV to secure a job, making a false tax declaration to save a company, and taking extra change from a cashier without returning it. These actions highlight ethical issues related to honesty and integrity.

3. **Social Norms and Deviations**: Some scenarios depict individuals acting against social norms or expectations, either through personal choice or as a form of protest. This includes refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance, making fun of one's political party, or using an old rotary phone instead of a modern one. These scenarios explore themes of individuality, rebellion, and societal expectations.

4. **Everyday Acts of Kindness or Rudeness**: A number of scenarios involve simple acts of kindness or rudeness that occur in daily life. These include helping pick up dropped papers, yelling at a delivery man for being late, or talking to a lonely child at a picnic. These actions reflect the small, yet significant, ways in which people interact with each other and the impact of those interactions.

5. **Personal and Professional Ethics**: Some scenarios focus on ethical dilemmas faced in professional settings, such as a lawyer trying to befriend a judge for personal gain or a financial adviser sharing confidential information. These scenarios highlight the challenges of maintaining ethical standards in professional life and the potential conflicts between personal interests and professional responsibilities.

6. **Cultural and Social Commentary**: A few scenarios provide commentary on cultural or social issues, such as a mayor trying to ban public displays of affection or someone making fun of their political party. These scenarios often reflect broader societal debates and the tension between personal freedoms and societal norms.

Each of these themes reflects different aspects of human behavior and moral decision-making, providing a rich tapestry of ethical considerations and social dynamics.
