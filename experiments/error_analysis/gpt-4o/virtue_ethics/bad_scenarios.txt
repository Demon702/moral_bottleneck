The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes based on the actions and moral dilemmas they depict:

1. **Sacrificing One to Save Many**: A significant number of scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic moral dilemma often referred to as the "trolley problem." Examples include pushing a person off a bridge to stop a train and save five workers, or diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Self-Interest and Dishonesty**: Many scenarios involve individuals acting out of self-interest, often resorting to dishonest or unethical behavior to achieve their goals. This includes lying on a CV to secure a job over more qualified candidates, or keeping extra change given by a cashier by mistake, knowing it will cause the cashier to suffer a loss.

3. **Breaking Rules or Laws**: Some scenarios depict individuals breaking rules or laws, either for personal gain or due to negligence. This includes driving the wrong way down a one-way street, or using a government calculator for personal audits and keeping it after leaving the job.

4. **Social Norms and Expectations**: Several scenarios highlight actions that go against social norms or expectations, which may or may not be morally wrong but are often seen as socially inappropriate. Examples include a woman burping loudly in public, or a college president singing a rival school's fight song.

5. **Acts of Courage or Defiance**: A few scenarios involve individuals standing up for their beliefs or taking courageous actions, even if they are controversial or unpopular. This includes refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance at a public event, or circulating a petition in support of equal pay for women.

6. **Manipulation and Influence**: Some scenarios involve individuals using manipulation or influence to gain an advantage, such as inviting a judge to lunch to gain favor in a legal case, or bribing a landlord to get an apartment repainted first.

These themes reflect a range of moral and ethical considerations, from life-and-death decisions to everyday ethical dilemmas, highlighting the complexity of human behavior and decision-making.
