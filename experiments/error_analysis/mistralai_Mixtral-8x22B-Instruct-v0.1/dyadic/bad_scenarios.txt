The scenarios can be grouped into several common themes based on the actions and moral dilemmas they present:

1. **Sacrificing One for Many**: A significant number of scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one person to save multiple others. This is a classic moral dilemma often referred to as the "trolley problem." Examples include pushing a person out of a helicopter to save others, or diverting a missile to save a family at the cost of another person's life.

2. **Deception and Dishonesty**: Several scenarios involve acts of deception or dishonesty for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes falsifying a CV to get a job, making a false tax declaration, or taking extra change from a cashier. These actions raise questions about the ethics of lying and deceit.

3. **Acts of Kindness and Helpfulness**: Some scenarios depict acts of kindness or helpfulness, such as helping a neighbor move a piano or involving a shy friend in social activities. These actions highlight the positive moral choices people can make to support and uplift others.

4. **Breaking Social Norms or Rules**: There are scenarios where individuals break social norms or rules, either intentionally or unintentionally. This includes refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance, or walking into the street without checking for traffic. These actions can provoke discussions about the importance and flexibility of societal rules.

5. **Moral Courage and Standing Up for Beliefs**: A few scenarios involve individuals standing up for their beliefs or taking a moral stand, such as circulating a petition for equal pay or joining the army despite having a religious opt-out. These actions demonstrate moral courage and the willingness to act according to one's principles.

6. **Everyday Life and Neutral Actions**: Many scenarios describe everyday actions that are morally neutral or mundane, such as ordering a favorite dish at a restaurant or singing loudly in the car. These scenarios serve as a contrast to the more morally charged situations and highlight the routine aspects of life.

Each of these themes reflects different aspects of human behavior and moral decision-making, providing a broad spectrum of ethical considerations.
