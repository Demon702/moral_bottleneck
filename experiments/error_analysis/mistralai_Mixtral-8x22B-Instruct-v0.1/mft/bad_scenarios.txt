1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice for the Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one or a few individuals to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the decision-maker must weigh the lives of a few against the lives of many. Examples include pushing a person off a bridge to stop a train and save five workers, or diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Self-Interest and Dishonesty**: Some scenarios depict individuals acting out of self-interest, often involving deceit or unethical behavior to gain personal advantage. This includes scenarios like making a false declaration about taxes to save a company from financial ruin, or inserting false information in a CV to secure a job over more qualified candidates.

3. **Moral Compromise in Extreme Situations**: Several scenarios involve individuals making morally questionable decisions under extreme circumstances, often involving life-and-death situations. These scenarios highlight the complexity of moral decision-making when faced with dire consequences. Examples include shooting an injured person to ensure the survival of a group in the Himalayas, or killing a tourist to convince terrorists to release hostages.

4. **Everyday Ethical Lapses**: Some scenarios illustrate minor ethical lapses or everyday moral decisions that people might face, such as taking extra change from a cashier without returning it, or bringing outside food into a movie theater due to financial constraints.

5. **Acts of Kindness or Consideration**: A few scenarios depict positive actions or behaviors that reflect kindness, consideration, or support for others. These include stopping to let another car into traffic or talking to a lonely child at a company picnic.

6. **Social and Cultural Norms**: Some scenarios reflect actions or statements that challenge or conform to social and cultural norms, such as a teacher hoping another school wins a contest or someone making a public post against their political party.

Each of these themes highlights different aspects of human behavior and moral reasoning, ranging from self-preservation and altruism to dishonesty and ethical compromise.
