The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different moral and ethical dilemmas or behaviors. Here are some of the common themes identified:

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice for the Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making a difficult decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include pushing a person off a bridge to stop a train and save five workers, or diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Dishonesty and Deception**: Several scenarios involve lying or deceit for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes falsifying a CV to get a job over more qualified candidates, or making a false tax declaration to save a company from financial trouble.

3. **Moral and Ethical Dilemmas in Crisis Situations**: Some scenarios depict high-pressure situations where individuals must make quick decisions that have moral implications, such as shooting an injured person to ensure the survival of others in a harsh environment, or diverting a missile to save a family at the cost of another life.

4. **Everyday Ethical Choices**: There are scenarios that reflect everyday ethical decisions, such as keeping extra change given by a cashier by mistake, or leaving a note after scratching someone’s car in a parking lot.

5. **Acts of Kindness and Consideration**: A few scenarios highlight positive behaviors, such as helping pick up dropped papers or talking to a lonely child at a picnic. These actions reflect empathy and consideration for others.

6. **Social and Cultural Norms**: Some scenarios involve actions that challenge or conform to social norms, such as a teacher demanding a student get a normal haircut, or a mother forcing her daughter to enroll in a specific college program.

7. **Personal Preferences and Habits**: There are scenarios that simply describe personal choices or habits, such as a person enjoying coffee at a café or someone singing along to music. These scenarios highlight individual preferences without moral implications.

Each of these themes reflects different aspects of human behavior and decision-making, ranging from ethical dilemmas to personal choices and societal norms.
