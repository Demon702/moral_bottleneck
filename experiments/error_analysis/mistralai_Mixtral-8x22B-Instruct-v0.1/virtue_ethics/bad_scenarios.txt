The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different moral and ethical dilemmas or behaviors. Here are some of the common themes identified:

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice for the Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making a difficult decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include pushing a person off a chairlift to save others, or diverting a trolley to kill one worker instead of five.

2. **Dishonesty and Deception**: Several scenarios involve lying or deceit for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes falsifying a CV to get a job, lying about money to a charity representative, or cheating in a card game with friends.

3. **Breaking the Law or Rules**: Some scenarios involve individuals knowingly breaking laws or rules, often for personal benefit or convenience. Examples include driving the wrong way down a one-way street or sneaking into a second movie without paying.

4. **Moral Courage and Confrontation**: A few scenarios depict individuals confronting others or standing up for their beliefs, even if it might lead to conflict. This includes confronting a friend about negative behavior or talking to a friend about menopause despite fear of backlash.

5. **Acts of Kindness or Support**: There are scenarios where individuals perform acts of kindness or support, often at personal risk or cost. Examples include talking to a lonely child at a picnic or covering a student's tuition fees.

6. **Public and Private Disloyalty**: Some scenarios involve acts of disloyalty or betrayal, either in public or private settings. This includes a college president singing a rival school's fight song or a person making fun of their political party online.

7. **Moral Ambiguity and Ethical Dilemmas**: Many scenarios present complex ethical dilemmas where the right course of action is not clear-cut, such as deciding whether to tell a friend about mean comments made behind their back or choosing to save a group of people at the expense of one individual.

Each of these themes highlights different aspects of human behavior and moral decision-making, illustrating the complexity and diversity of ethical challenges people face in various situations.
