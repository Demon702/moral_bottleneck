The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different aspects of human behavior and moral dilemmas.

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice**: A significant number of scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one person to save multiple others. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include the helicopter pilot pushing a person out to save others, and the head zookeeper pushing a keeper to save visitors from a lion.

2. **Moral and Ethical Dilemmas**: Many scenarios present situations where individuals face ethical choices, often involving lying or deceit to achieve a perceived greater good or personal benefit. For instance, the person who makes a false declaration on their tax return to save their company, or the individual who lies about their computer skills on a CV to secure a job.

3. **Acts of Kindness and Helpfulness**: Some scenarios depict acts of kindness or helpfulness, where individuals go out of their way to assist others. Examples include helping a neighbor move a piano and stopping to let another car into the line of traffic.

4. **Everyday Decisions and Mistakes**: Several scenarios involve mundane, everyday decisions or mistakes that people make, which may or may not have moral implications. These include forgetting a wedding anniversary or accidentally knocking a glass off a table.

5. **Social and Cultural Norms**: A few scenarios touch on social and cultural norms, where individuals either conform to or defy societal expectations. For example, someone refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance or a woman wearing a large sun hat indoors.

6. **Personal Relationships and Conflicts**: Some scenarios explore personal relationships and the conflicts that arise within them, such as forgiving a cheating partner or dealing with family disapproval over a marriage decision.

7. **Deception and Dishonesty**: There are scenarios where individuals engage in deception or dishonesty for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes lying about vacation days at work or keeping extra change given by a cashier.

8. **Moral Courage and Integrity**: A few scenarios highlight moral courage and integrity, where individuals make difficult choices that align with their values, such as circulating a petition for equal pay or helping students pay for their classes.

Each of these themes reflects different facets of human behavior, illustrating the complexity of moral decision-making and the diverse motivations that drive people's actions.
