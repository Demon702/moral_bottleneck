The scenarios presented often involve moral dilemmas where a decision must be made to sacrifice one or a few individuals to save a larger group. This is a classic ethical problem known as the "trolley problem," where one must choose between actively causing harm to save more people or allowing harm to occur to a larger group. For example, in the scenario where a person diverts a trolley onto a secondary track to save five workers, knowing it will kill one worker, and the scenario where a commander launches false targets to divert a torpedo, sacrificing a patrol boat to save a larger group, both illustrate this moral quandary.

Another common theme is the use of deception or dishonesty to achieve a desired outcome, often for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This is seen in scenarios such as the person who inserts false information in their CV to secure a job over more qualified candidates, and the shop owner who makes a false tax declaration to avoid financial loss. These scenarios highlight ethical issues related to integrity and honesty.

Some scenarios involve taking advantage of others or exploiting situations for personal benefit, often at the expense of fairness or justice. For instance, the scenario where a person keeps extra change given by a distracted cashier, knowing she will have to reimburse the till, and the scenario where someone lies to a school about students paying for classes to help them, both demonstrate this theme of exploitation or manipulation.

Additionally, there are scenarios that involve making difficult decisions under pressure, often with life-or-death consequences. These scenarios require quick thinking and prioritization of lives, such as the fireman who activates fire doors to save six people at the cost of one, and the commander who orders a plane to be shot down to save others, knowing the pilot will die. These highlight the moral complexity and emotional burden of making such decisions.

Lastly, some scenarios depict acts of cruelty or harm towards others, either as a means to an end or as a result of personal grievances. For example, the scenario where someone throws an acquaintance out of their home for disagreeing with them, and the one where a person ridicules others behind their backs, both illustrate this theme of interpersonal conflict and harm.
