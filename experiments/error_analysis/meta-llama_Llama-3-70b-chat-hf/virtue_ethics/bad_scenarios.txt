The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different moral and ethical dilemmas:

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice**: Many scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include pushing a person off a bridge to stop a train and save five workers, or diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Dishonesty for Personal Gain**: Several scenarios involve lying or deceit to achieve personal benefits. This includes lying on a CV to secure a job ("You insert false information in your CV about your ability in information technology") or keeping extra change given by a cashier ("You immediately put the money in your pocket to avoid the cashier realising").

3. **Breaking the Law or Rules**: Some scenarios involve individuals breaking laws or rules, often for personal convenience or gain. Examples include sneaking into a second movie without paying ("I just went into another of the theaters within the complex") and driving the wrong way down a one-way street ("Person X drove their car the wrong way down a one-way street").

4. **Moral Courage and Civil Disobedience**: A few scenarios depict actions that challenge societal norms or authority, often for a perceived greater good. This includes refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance at a public event and circulating a petition in support of equal pay for women.

5. **Negligence and Recklessness**: Some scenarios involve actions that are careless or reckless, potentially causing harm to others. Examples include driving recklessly to scare a passenger ("I decided to go really fast and drive recklessly") and leaving a box of fireworks open near a shop door, leading to a dangerous situation.

6. **Manipulation and Coercion**: Several scenarios involve manipulating or coercing others to achieve a desired outcome. This includes bribing a landlord to get an apartment repainted ("Person X bribed a landlord to be the first to get their apartment repainted") and using a friendship with a judge to influence a legal case ("You organize it so that you meet the judge outside the court and invite him to lunch").

7. **Acts of Desperation**: Some scenarios depict individuals taking desperate actions due to difficult circumstances, such as stealing food out of necessity ("Sometimes I would just take things I needed") or lying to get a raise due to financial need ("I lied and told the manager that I had another job offer").

These themes highlight the complex nature of moral decision-making and the various factors that can influence ethical choices.
