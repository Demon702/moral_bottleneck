The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, primarily revolving around moral dilemmas, ethical decision-making, and everyday actions. Here are the common themes identified:

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice for Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making a difficult decision to sacrifice one or a few individuals to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the decision-maker must weigh the lives of a few against the many. Examples include pushing a stranger under a tram to save five people crossing the road, and diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Moral and Ethical Breaches for Personal Gain**: Some scenarios involve individuals making unethical choices for personal benefit, often at the expense of others. This includes actions like making a false tax declaration to save a company from financial ruin, or inserting false information in a CV to secure a job over more qualified candidates.

3. **Everyday Ethical Decisions**: A few scenarios depict everyday situations where individuals face minor ethical decisions, such as deciding whether to report a car accident in a parking lot or keeping extra change given by a cashier. These scenarios highlight the moral choices people make in daily life, like deciding whether to smoke in someone else's apartment or demanding a refund for slow service at a restaurant.

4. **Acts of Kindness and Support**: Some scenarios involve helping others without any moral conflict, such as helping a neighbor move a piano or talking to a lonely child at a picnic. These actions reflect positive social behavior and empathy.

5. **Personal Achievements and Skills**: A number of scenarios describe personal accomplishments or skills, such as winning an award at a karaoke event or learning a secret pie recipe. These scenarios focus on individual growth and success.

6. **Interpersonal Relationships and Communication**: A few scenarios involve navigating personal relationships and communication, such as discussing sensitive topics with friends or family, or dealing with a partner's deception. These scenarios highlight the complexities of human interactions and the importance of honesty and understanding.

Each of these themes reflects different aspects of human behavior and decision-making, ranging from life-and-death moral dilemmas to everyday ethical choices and personal achievements.
