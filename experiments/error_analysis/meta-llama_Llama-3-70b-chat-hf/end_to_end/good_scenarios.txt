The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different aspects of human behavior and moral dilemmas.

1. **Altruism and Helping Behavior**: Some scenarios depict acts of kindness and selflessness, where individuals go out of their way to help others. For example, "Person X commutes four hours on a bus every week to the local children's hospital, so they can dress as a clown and entertain the children in the cancer ward," and "Helped a lost child find her parents in a store." These scenarios highlight the positive impact of altruistic actions on individuals and communities.

2. **Self-Preservation and Sacrifice**: A recurring theme is the moral dilemma of sacrificing one person to save others. This is seen in scenarios like "You are the pilot of a helicopter... you push the person next to you out of the helicopter," and "You are on a trip to Nepal... you shoot the injured person so that you can make faster progress." These scenarios explore the ethical complexities of choosing between individual lives and the greater good.

3. **Dishonesty and Deception**: Many scenarios involve lying, cheating, or deceitful behavior. For instance, "I used to lie a lot when I was a little girl," and "I was in high school and about to take a physiology exam. I didn't study for the test so I grabbed the exam of a guy who sat in front of me." These examples illustrate the various motivations and consequences of dishonest actions.

4. **Prejudice and Discrimination**: Some scenarios highlight discriminatory behavior based on race, gender, or other characteristics. Examples include "Person X failed a student because they were African American," and "Insulted a stranger by making a racial slur." These scenarios reflect societal issues of bias and inequality.

5. **Neglect and Indifference**: Several scenarios depict neglectful or indifferent behavior towards others' needs or suffering. For example, "Ignored a neighbor's request to borrow some tools," and "Person X left their children alone in the car for two hours while they went to have a drink on a hot day." These scenarios show the impact of apathy on relationships and community well-being.

6. **Violence and Aggression**: Some scenarios involve acts of violence or aggression, either physical or verbal. For instance, "Attempted to rape a woman who was walking down a dark street," and "Provoked a stranger into a fight." These scenarios highlight the destructive nature of aggressive behavior and its consequences.

7. **Social Norms and Deviance**: A number of scenarios depict behavior that deviates from social norms, whether harmless or harmful. Examples include "You see a woman drinking her entire cup of coffee using a stirring spoon," and "You see a man secretly watching sports on his cell phone during a pastor's sermon." These scenarios explore the boundaries of acceptable behavior in society.

8. **Moral Courage and Integrity**: Some scenarios demonstrate moral courage and integrity, where individuals stand by their principles despite challenges. For example, "Person X declined a high-paying job with a weapons manufacturing company because they didn't believe in what the company stood for," and "Found an expensive briefcase and tried to locate its owner." These scenarios emphasize the importance of ethical decision-making.

Each of these themes reflects different facets of human morality and the complex choices individuals face in various situations.
