The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different moral and ethical dilemmas or behaviors:

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice for the Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making a difficult decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include pushing a person out of a helicopter to save others, or diverting a train to kill one person instead of five.

2. **Deception and Dishonesty**: Several scenarios involve lying or deceit for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes lying about being in a union to get better treatment, or falsifying a CV to secure a job. These scenarios highlight the moral implications of dishonesty.

3. **Moral Courage and Confrontation**: Some scenarios involve confronting others or standing up for one's beliefs, even if it leads to conflict or discomfort. This includes confronting a friend about their behavior or refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance. These scenarios explore the theme of moral courage.

4. **Self-Interest and Exploitation**: A number of scenarios depict individuals acting out of self-interest, often at the expense of others. This includes using confidential information for financial gain or exploiting a friendship for personal benefit. These scenarios examine the ethical implications of self-serving actions.

5. **Cultural and Social Norms**: Some scenarios involve actions that challenge or conform to societal norms and expectations. This includes participating in a controversial rally or burning a national flag. These scenarios explore the tension between individual actions and societal values.

6. **Everyday Decisions and Mistakes**: There are scenarios that depict mundane or everyday decisions, some of which involve minor ethical breaches or mistakes. This includes accidentally scratching a car and leaving without reporting it, or using milk chocolate instead of dark chocolate in a recipe. These scenarios highlight the moral considerations in everyday life.

Each of these themes presents a different aspect of human behavior and moral decision-making, illustrating the complexity and diversity of ethical dilemmas people face.
