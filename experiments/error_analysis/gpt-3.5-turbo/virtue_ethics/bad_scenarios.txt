The scenarios can be categorized by a few common themes, particularly focusing on moral dilemmas, acts of kindness or selfishness, and questions of legality or propriety.

1. **Moral Dilemmas and Sacrificial Decisions:** Many scenarios present ethical decisions where the protagonist must choose between sacrificing one or a few individuals to save a larger number of people. A classic example is the "trolley problem," represented here by scenarios such as diverting a trolley onto a secondary track to save five workers while knowingly killing one, or pushing a person into the cogs of a machine to save several others. These scenarios typically involve difficult decisions where the outcome results in one or a few individuals suffering to prevent greater harm. Another scenario highlighting this is diverting a toxic fume towards a room with one patient to save others.

2. **Acts of Kindness or Selflessness:** Several scenarios depict acts of kindness or altruism where individuals go out of their way to help others without any immediate benefit to themselves. Examples include volunteering time to save seals or pushing aside personal discomfort to assist a friend or family member in need, such as shaving one's head in solidarity with a partner undergoing cancer treatment.

3. **Selfish or Egotistical Actions:** Some scenarios involve actions that are primarily selfish or prioritize personal benefit and convenience over responsibility or consideration for others. Examples include taking extra change from a cashier's mistake without correction or ratting out a sibling for stealing. They often demonstrate a lack of regard for social or moral consequences in pursuit of personal gain or avoidance of discomfort.

4. **Legal and Propriety Issues:** There are scenarios that involve legal or propriety breaches, where actions might not be illegal but are frowned upon or extend beyond ethical norms. Examples include crossing over to another movie theater without paying for a second film, or lying about gifts or charitable donations for personal benefit. These scenarios challenge the social constructs of acceptable behavior and the consequences of stepping outside those bounds.

Together, these categories reflect the complexities of human decisions in various contexts, highlighting the tension between ethical principles, personal interest, and societal norms. Each example challenges the protagonist to weigh the consequences of their actions, often placing them in situations without clear right or wrong answers.
