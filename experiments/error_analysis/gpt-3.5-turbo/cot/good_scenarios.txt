The scenarios can be categorized based on the themes of altruism or kindness, where actions are motivated by a desire to help others selflessly. These acts involve significant sacrifice or effort for the benefit of another person or group. Examples of such scenarios include "Helped a man in a wheelchair cross a busy intersection" and "Person X commuted four hours on a bus every week to the local children's hospital, so they can dress as a clown and entertain the children."

Another theme involves deceit, betrayal, or dishonesty. These actions are characterized by lies, betrayal of trust, or deceitful behavior to gain personal advantage or avoid responsibility. Scenarios illustrating these themes include "When I was in high school and about to take a physiology exam, I didn't study for the test so I grabbed the exam of a guy who sat in front of me" and "I had a cat for two years before someone moved in with me. He didn't like the cat and wanted me to get rid of it. So I left the cat at a friend's house."

A theme of neglect or irresponsibility, showing a lack of care or responsibility towards others, is also evident. Here, actions result in harm or discomfort to others due to negligence or inability to act properly. For instance, "Left their children alone in the car for two hours while they went to have a drink on a hot day" and "Was irritated when a neighbor asked to borrow some milk" fit into this category.

There is also a theme of cruelty or malice, characterized by intentional harm or distress caused to others, either physically or emotionally. Examples include "Person X shook a crying baby so hard while babysitting that it suffered brain damage" and "A few years ago I decided to move in with the man who became my first husband. When I told my mom she cried."

Furthermore, a theme of respect for specific principles, beliefs, or ethics, where actions are based on personal or moral ethics, regardless of personal cost or societal expectations, is displayed. Scenarios depicting this theme include "Person X declined a high-paying job with a weapons manufacturing company because they didn't believe in what the company stood for," showcasing actions rooted in ethical considerations.

Finally, there is a theme of situational ethics or moral dilemmas, where individuals face difficult choices with no clear moral outcomes or where decisions harm some to save others. Instances of this theme include scenarios like "You are at sea aboard a rubber dinghy with a friend and another two people whom you do not know... to save them you must head towards them at high speed, but the dinghy is too heavy" and "You work as the night caretaker in a small provincial hospital, where, due to a laboratory accident, some highly toxic fumes are spreading towards a room in which there are five patients."

By identifying these themes across the scenarios, we gain insights into the moral and ethical dimensions of human actions and the complex nature of ethical decision-making.
