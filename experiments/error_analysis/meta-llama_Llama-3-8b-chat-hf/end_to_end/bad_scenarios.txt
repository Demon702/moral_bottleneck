The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes based on the actions and moral dilemmas they depict. Here are some of the themes identified:

1. **Moral Dilemmas and Sacrifices for the Greater Good**: Many scenarios involve making a difficult decision where one person's life or well-being is sacrificed to save multiple others. This is a classic moral dilemma often referred to as the "trolley problem." Examples include pushing a stranger under a tram to save five people and diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Acts of Dishonesty or Deception**: Several scenarios involve lying, cheating, or deceiving others for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes falsifying a CV to get a job over more qualified candidates and making a false tax declaration to save a company from financial trouble.

3. **Acts of Courage or Heroism**: Some scenarios depict individuals taking bold actions to save others, often at great personal risk. These acts are typically seen as heroic, such as a fireman saving people trapped in a skyscraper fire or a secret service agent preventing an assassination.

4. **Social Norms and Rebellion**: A number of scenarios involve individuals defying social norms or authority figures, either as a form of protest or personal expression. Examples include refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance and a woman refusing to stand when a judge enters the courtroom.

5. **Everyday Life and Personal Achievements**: Many scenarios describe mundane activities or personal achievements that do not involve moral dilemmas or significant ethical considerations. These include Person X winning an award at a karaoke event and learning a new language.

6. **Acts of Kindness or Empathy**: Some scenarios highlight acts of kindness, empathy, or support towards others, such as talking to a lonely child at a company picnic or covering a student's tuition fees.

7. **Selfish or Harmful Actions**: There are scenarios where individuals act selfishly or harmfully towards others, often for personal benefit or out of negligence. Examples include siphoning gas from a car and laughing at someone who trips and falls.

These themes reflect a wide range of human behaviors and ethical considerations, from selfless heroism to selfish deceit, and from everyday achievements to significant moral dilemmas.
