The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes based on the actions and moral dilemmas they depict:

1. **Sacrificing One to Save Many**: A significant number of scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic moral dilemma often referred to as the "trolley problem." Examples include pushing a person under a tram to save five others, or shooting a pilot to prevent a plane from crashing into a control tower.

2. **Acts of Dishonesty or Deception**: Many scenarios involve individuals engaging in dishonest or deceptive behavior for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes actions like making a false tax declaration to save a company from financial ruin, or keeping extra change given by mistake at a supermarket. 

3. **Breaking Social Norms or Expectations**: Some scenarios depict individuals breaking social norms or expectations, which may or may not have moral implications. Examples include a woman wearing a large sun hat indoors or a man using a rotary phone instead of a modern one.

4. **Acts of Kindness or Helpfulness**: There are scenarios where individuals perform acts of kindness or helpfulness, such as helping a neighbor move a piano or putting money in a parking meter for an unknown driver.

5. **Acts of Rudeness or Insensitivity**: Several scenarios involve individuals being rude or insensitive to others, such as laughing at someone who trips or loudly criticizing art at a children's show.

6. **Moral Courage or Standing Up for Beliefs**: Some scenarios involve individuals standing up for their beliefs or showing moral courage, such as refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance or burning a flag in protest.

7. **Everyday Life and Mundane Activities**: A number of scenarios simply depict everyday life activities that do not necessarily have moral implications, such as ordering a favorite dish from a restaurant or singing along to a song in the car.

Each of these themes highlights different aspects of human behavior and moral decision-making, ranging from life-and-death dilemmas to everyday choices and social interactions.
