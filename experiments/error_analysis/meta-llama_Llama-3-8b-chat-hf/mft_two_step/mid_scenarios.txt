The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes based on the actions and moral implications involved. 

One prominent theme is **sacrificing one for the many**, where an individual makes a decision to harm or sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is evident in scenarios such as the helicopter pilot pushing a person out to save others, or the foreman throwing a worker off a platform to save the rest. These scenarios often involve a moral dilemma where the decision-maker must weigh the value of one life against several others.

Another theme is **acts of kindness and altruism**, where individuals go out of their way to help others without expecting anything in return. Examples include helping an older man carry his luggage to his car and offering to help a neighbor fix a fence. These actions reflect a selfless concern for the well-being of others and highlight the positive impact of empathy and generosity.

A contrasting theme is **selfishness and deceit**, where individuals act in their own interest, often at the expense of others. This includes scenarios like lying on a CV to gain employment over more qualified candidates or cheating on a partner. These actions are characterized by a disregard for honesty and integrity, prioritizing personal gain over ethical considerations.

There is also a theme of **moral negligence and indifference**, where individuals fail to act or choose to ignore situations that require intervention. For instance, ignoring a woman's cry for help in lifting a baby carriage or not calling an ambulance for a stabbing victim. These scenarios highlight the consequences of inaction and the moral responsibility to assist those in need.

Lastly, there are **everyday moral decisions and honesty**, where individuals face choices that test their integrity in daily life. Examples include returning a lost wallet to its owner or telling a store owner about receiving too much change. These scenarios emphasize the importance of honesty and the impact of seemingly small decisions on personal and societal ethics.
