The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different aspects of human behavior and moral dilemmas.

1. **Exploitation and Taking Advantage**: Some scenarios involve individuals taking advantage of others for personal gain or convenience. For example, the scenario where a friend exploits another's help during a move by not contributing to gas or expressing gratitude, and the one where someone uses a work calculator at home and keeps it after leaving the job, both illustrate exploitation of resources or goodwill.

2. **Moral Dilemmas and Sacrifices**: Many scenarios involve making difficult decisions where one life is sacrificed to save others. These include situations like pushing someone out of a helicopter to save others or diverting a missile to save a family at the cost of another life. These scenarios highlight the ethical challenges of making life-and-death decisions.

3. **Dishonesty and Deception**: Several scenarios involve lying or deceit for personal benefit, such as lying about age to gain entry into a dance school or falsifying a CV to secure a job. These actions reflect the moral implications of dishonesty in various contexts.

4. **Neglect and Indifference**: Some scenarios depict neglect or indifference towards others' needs or feelings. For instance, ignoring a neighbor's request to borrow tools or refusing to help a classmate with homework shows a lack of empathy or willingness to assist others.

5. **Social Norms and Deviance**: There are scenarios where individuals act against social norms or expectations, such as refusing to stand for the pledge of allegiance or a woman avoiding sitting next to an obese person on the bus. These actions challenge societal norms and provoke discussions about conformity and individual rights.

6. **Selfishness and Greed**: Instances of selfish behavior are evident in scenarios like keeping extra change given by mistake or taking more than one's share of resources, such as a manager taking half the doughnuts. These actions highlight the moral issues surrounding greed and self-interest.

7. **Courage and Altruism**: A few scenarios depict acts of courage or altruism, such as talking to a lonely child at a company picnic or stopping to let another car into traffic. These actions demonstrate positive moral behavior and the impact of kindness and empathy.

8. **Judgment and Prejudice**: Some scenarios involve judgment or prejudice, such as a girl laughing at her friend's dad being a janitor or a teacher ordering a student to get a normal haircut. These situations reflect societal biases and the impact of judgmental attitudes.

Each of these themes provides insight into the complexities of human behavior and the moral choices individuals face in various situations.
