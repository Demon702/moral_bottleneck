The scenarios presented can be grouped into several common themes, each illustrating different moral and ethical dilemmas or behaviors:

1. **Utilitarian Sacrifice**: Many scenarios involve making a decision to sacrifice one person to save a larger group. This is a classic utilitarian dilemma where the needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few. Examples include pushing a person out of a helicopter to save others, or diverting toxic fumes to a room with one patient to save five others.

2. **Deception and Dishonesty**: Several scenarios involve lying or deceit for personal gain or to avoid negative consequences. This includes lying about one's age to get into dance school, falsifying a CV to get a job, or lying to a spouse about one's whereabouts.

3. **Selfishness and Exploitation**: Some scenarios depict individuals taking advantage of others or acting selfishly. This includes a friend exploiting another's help during a move without gratitude, or someone taking extra change from a cashier without returning it.

4. **Moral Courage and Cowardice**: A few scenarios highlight the choice between standing up for what is right versus avoiding confrontation or responsibility. This includes ignoring a partner's illegal activities or refusing to help someone in need.

5. **Social and Cultural Norms**: Some scenarios involve actions that challenge or conform to societal norms, such as refusing to stand for a judge or burning a national flag. These actions often provoke questions about respect, tradition, and personal freedom.

6. **Ethical Dilemmas in Professional Settings**: Several scenarios involve ethical challenges in professional environments, such as a financial adviser sharing confidential information or a medical team making life-and-death decisions in the desert.

7. **Interpersonal Relationships and Conflicts**: Many scenarios revolve around personal relationships and the conflicts that arise within them, such as a friend being jealous of another's relationships or a person refusing to attend a disliked relative's funeral.

8. **Acts of Kindness and Generosity**: A few scenarios depict acts of kindness, such as a teacher covering students' fees or someone always remembering friends' birthdays, contrasting with the more negative actions in other scenarios.

Each of these themes reflects different aspects of human behavior and the complex moral decisions people face in various situations.
