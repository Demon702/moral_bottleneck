Utility is the property of something whereby it tends to produce benefit, advantage, pleasure, good, or happiness, or to prevent the happening of mischief, pain, evil, or unhappiness to the party whose interest is considered. If that party is the community in general, then the happiness of the community; if it’s a particular individual, then the happiness of that individual.
An action is said to conform to the principle of utility when its tendency to increase the happiness of the community is greater than any tendency it has to lessen it.
